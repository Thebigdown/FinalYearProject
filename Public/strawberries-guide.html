Strawberry Growing Guide

Complete Guide to Growing Strawberries

Learn how to grow sweet, juicy strawberries in containers and hanging baskets - perfect for small spaces and urban gardens.

Step 1: Choosing Strawberry Types

Select the right strawberry type and varieties for your container growing setup.

Strawberry Types:

  • June-bearing: One large crop in late spring/early summer
  • Ever-bearing: Two to three smaller crops throughout season
  • Day-neutral: Continuous production all season long
  • Alpine: Small, intensely flavored, continuous harvest

Best Container Varieties:

  • Day-neutral: Seascape, Albion, San Andreas (continuous harvest)
  • Ever-bearing: Ozark Beauty, Quinault, Fort Laramie
  • Hanging Basket: Temptation, Lipstick, Tristan
  • Alpine: Mignonette, Alexandria, Rügen (compact, flavorful)

Container-Friendly Features:

  • Compact growth habit
  • High productivity per plant
  • Disease resistance
  • Good flavor and texture
  • Extended harvest period

Special Container Types:

  • Hanging Baskets: Trailing varieties with runners
  • Strawberry Towers: Vertical growing systems
  • Window Boxes: Compact varieties in shallow containers
  • Strawberry Jars: Traditional tiered planters

Step 2: Container & Soil Setup

Prepare the right containers and soil mix for healthy strawberry growth.

Container Requirements:

  • Minimum 8 inches deep for adequate root space
  • 12+ inches wide for multiple plants
  • Excellent drainage holes (strawberries hate wet feet)
  • Wide, shallow containers work better than deep ones

Container Options:

  • Standard Pots: 12-inch diameter for 3-4 plants
  • Window Boxes: 8 inches deep, space plants 8 inches apart
  • Hanging Baskets: 14+ inches diameter, lined with coco fiber
  • Strawberry Towers: Vertical planters with multiple pockets

Soil Mix:

  • Well-draining potting soil as base
  • Add compost (25% of volume)
  • Mix in perlite or coarse sand for drainage
  • pH between 5.5-6.5 (slightly acidic)
  • Rich in organic matter

Growing Conditions:

  • Light: 6-8 hours direct sunlight daily
  • Temperature: Cool weather preferred, 60-80°F (15-27°C)
  • Air Circulation: Good airflow prevents fungal diseases
  • Protection: Shelter from strong winds and extreme heat

Drainage Considerations:

  • Add gravel or broken pottery to bottom of containers
  • Ensure water can drain freely
  • Elevate containers slightly for better drainage
  • Check drainage holes regularly to prevent clogging

Step 3: Planting Methods

Plant strawberries using transplants or runners for best container results.

Planting Transplants:

  1. Purchase certified disease-free plants from reputable sources
  2. Soak roots in water for 30 minutes before planting
  3. Dig holes large enough for root system
  4. Plant with crown at soil level (not buried)
  5. Firm soil gently around roots
  6. Water thoroughly after planting

Crown Planting Depth:

  • Too Deep: Crown buried, plant may rot
  • Too Shallow: Roots exposed, plant dries out
  • Just Right: Crown level with soil surface
  • Crown is where leaves emerge from roots

Spacing Guidelines:

  • Standard Containers: 8-12 inches between plants
  • Hanging Baskets: 6-8 inches apart
  • Window Boxes: Single row, 8 inches apart
  • Strawberry Towers: One plant per pocket

Timing:

  • Spring: Plant after last frost danger passes
  • Fall: Plant 6-8 weeks before first frost
  • Indoor/Greenhouse: Plant any time
  • Cool, cloudy days are best for transplanting

From Runners:

  • Use runners from healthy, productive plants
  • Allow runner to root while still attached to parent
  • Cut from parent once rooted
  • Transplant immediately to new container

Step 4: Early Care & Establishment

Provide proper care during the critical establishment period.

Watering:

  • Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged
  • Water at soil level to avoid wetting leaves
  • Check containers daily in hot weather
  • Water early morning for best disease prevention
  • Reduce watering in fall as growth slows

First Year Flower Removal:

  • Remove all flowers first 6-8 weeks after planting
  • Allows plants to establish strong root system
  • Results in better production later
  • Day-neutral varieties: remove flowers first month only
  • Pinch flowers as soon as they appear

Runner Management:

  • For fruit production: Remove all runners
  • For plant propagation: Allow some runners to develop
  • Cut runners with clean scissors
  • Redirect plant energy into fruit production

Mulching:

  • Apply 2-inch layer of organic mulch
  • Keep mulch away from plant crowns
  • Use straw, pine needles, or shredded leaves
  • Helps retain moisture and suppress weeds
  • Keeps fruit clean and off soil

Protection:

  • Protect from birds with netting
  • Provide shade during extreme heat
  • Shield from strong winds
  • Use copper tape or diatomaceous earth for slug control
  • Hand-pick any pests found on plants

Step 5: Fertilizing & Maintenance

Maintain healthy plants with proper fertilizing and ongoing care.

Fertilizing Schedule:

  • Early Spring: Balanced organic fertilizer (10-10-10) when growth starts
  • After Harvest: Light application of balanced organic fertilizer
  • Fall: Phosphorus and potassium, reduce nitrogen
  • Container Plants: Feed every 2-3 weeks during growing season

Organic Feeding Options:

  • Compost worked into soil surface
  • Fish emulsion for quick nitrogen boost (check local regulations)
  • Bone meal for phosphorus
  • Kelp meal for trace minerals
  • Compost tea applied bi-weekly

Leaf Care:

  • Remove old, yellowing, or diseased leaves regularly
  • Trim leaves that touch the ground
  • Maintain good air circulation around plants
  • Clean up fallen leaves and debris

Common Problems & Solutions:

  • Powdery mildew: White coating on leaves (improve air circulation)
  • Gray mold: Fuzzy gray growth on fruit (reduce humidity)
  • Aphids: Small insects on leaves (spray with insecticidal soap)
  • Spider mites: Fine webbing on leaves (spray with horticultural oil)
  • General pest control: Use companion planting with herbs like thyme or sage

Seasonal Care:

  • Spring: Remove winter protection, clean up debris
  • Summer: Regular watering, harvesting, runner removal
  • Fall: Reduce watering, clean up diseased leaves
  • Winter: Protect containers from freezing

Step 6: Harvesting & Storage

Harvest at peak ripeness for best flavor and learn proper storage methods.

When to Harvest:

  • Full Color: Completely red with no white areas
  • Soft Texture: Slight give when gently pressed
  • Sweet Aroma: Strong strawberry fragrance
  • Easy Removal: Fruit separates easily from plant
  • Harvest every 1-2 days during peak season

Harvesting Method:

  1. Pick in cool morning when possible
  2. Grasp berry gently and twist
  3. Leave green cap (calyx) attached
  4. Place carefully in shallow containers
  5. Handle minimally to avoid bruising
  6. Don't wash until ready to eat

Harvest Tips:

  • Pick fully ripe berries only (won't ripen off plant)
  • Remove overripe or damaged fruit immediately
  • Harvest regularly to encourage more production
  • Use clean hands or tools
  • Keep berries cool after picking

Storage Methods:

  • Fresh: Refrigerate unwashed for 3-7 days
  • Freezing: Wash, hull, and freeze on trays
  • Preserving: Make jam, jelly, or syrup
  • Dehydrating: Dry for strawberry chips

Using Your Strawberries:

  • Fresh eating and desserts
  • Smoothies and strawberry milk
  • Jams, jellies, and preserves
  • Strawberry shortcake and pies
  • Frozen for year-round use

End of Season Care:

  • Continue harvesting until frost
  • Clean up all plant debris
  • Apply winter protection if needed
  • Move containers to protected area in cold climates
  • Plan for next year's planting

Winter Protection:

  • Mulch heavily around containers
  • Wrap containers with insulation
  • Move to unheated garage or shed
  • Water occasionally if soil completely dry
