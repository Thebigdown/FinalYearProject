Potato Growing Guide

Complete Guide to Growing Potatoes

Learn how to grow nutritious potatoes in containers and bags - perfect for urban spaces with limited room.

Step 1: Choosing Potato Varieties

Select potato varieties suited for container growing and your climate.

Best Container Varieties:

  • Early Varieties: Yukon Gold, Red Pontiac, Irish Cobbler (60-70 days)
  • Fingerlings: Russian Banana, Purple Peruvian, French Fingerling (compact)
  • Small Potatoes: Baby potatoes, New potatoes (space-efficient)
  • Specialty: Purple Majesty, All Blue, German Butterball

Container-Friendly Traits:

  • Compact growth habit
  • Early maturity (60-90 days)
  • Good yield in small spaces
  • Disease resistance

Seed Potato Sources:

  • Buy certified disease-free seed potatoes from reputable suppliers
  • Check local regulations for potato imports if ordering online
  • Avoid grocery store potatoes (often treated with growth inhibitors)
  • Cut large seed potatoes into pieces with 2-3 eyes
  • Let cut pieces dry for 24-48 hours before planting

Step 2: Container Setup

Prepare containers that allow for proper potato development and harvesting.

Container Options:

  • Grow Bags: 15-20 gallon fabric bags (best drainage)
  • Trash Cans: Clean, with drainage holes
  • Buckets: 5-gallon minimum per plant
  • Wooden Boxes: Build custom containers

Container Requirements:

  • Minimum 15 gallons for good yield
  • At least 18-24 inches deep
  • Excellent drainage (multiple holes)
  • Easy access for harvesting

Soil Mix:

  • Well-draining potting mix
  • Add compost (30% of volume)
  • Mix in perlite for drainage
  • pH between 5.5-6.5 (slightly acidic)
  • Avoid heavy clay or compacted soil

Growing Conditions:

  • Light: 6-8 hours direct sunlight
  • Temperature: 60-70°F (15-21°C) optimal
  • Location: Cool, well-ventilated area

Step 3: Planting Process

Plant your seed potatoes using the layering method for maximum yield.

Planting Steps:

  1. Fill container with 6 inches of soil mix
  2. Place 2-3 seed potato pieces, eyes facing up
  3. Cover with 4 inches of soil
  4. Water thoroughly but gently
  5. Mark the container with variety and date

Timing:

  • Spring Planting: 2-3 weeks before last frost
  • Fall Planting: 10-12 weeks before first frost
  • Indoor Growing: Year-round with proper temperature

Spacing:

  • One seed potato per 5-gallon container
  • 2-3 pieces per 15-20 gallon container
  • Space pieces 8-12 inches apart

The Hilling Process:

  • Add soil as plants grow to cover stems
  • Leave top 4-6 inches of leaves exposed
  • Repeat every 2-3 weeks until container is full
  • This encourages more potato formation

Step 4: Care & Maintenance

Provide consistent care for healthy growth and maximum harvest.

Watering:

  • Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged
  • Water when top 2 inches of soil feel dry
  • Reduce watering 2 weeks before harvest
  • Avoid overhead watering (can cause diseases)
  • Use drip irrigation or water at soil level

Fertilizing:

  • Planting: Mix balanced organic fertilizer into soil
  • Growing: Side-dress with compost every 3 weeks
  • Flowering: Reduce nitrogen, increase potassium
  • Avoid over-fertilizing (causes more leaves, fewer potatoes)

Hilling and Mulching:

  • Continue adding soil as plants grow
  • Never let developing potatoes see sunlight (turns green and toxic)
  • Mulch around containers to retain moisture
  • Keep soil loose for proper tuber development

Pest and Disease Management:

  • Watch for Colorado potato beetles - hand-pick if found
  • Monitor for aphids and flea beetles
  • Use universally approved methods: insecticidal soap or horticultural oil
  • Apply diatomaceous earth around plants for crawling pests
  • Ensure good air circulation to prevent fungal diseases
  • Remove any diseased foliage immediately
  • Practice crop rotation yearly to prevent disease buildup
  • For serious infestations, consult local garden centers for regionally-approved treatments

Step 5: Harvesting

Learn when and how to harvest for the best quality potatoes.

Harvest Timing:

  • New Potatoes: 2-3 weeks after flowering begins
  • Mature Potatoes: When foliage begins to yellow and die
  • Storage Potatoes: Wait 2-3 weeks after foliage dies back
  • Harvest before first hard frost

Harvesting Method:

  1. Stop watering 1-2 weeks before harvest
  2. Gently dump container contents onto tarp
  3. Carefully sort through soil to find all potatoes
  4. Brush off excess soil (don't wash immediately)
  5. Handle gently to avoid bruising

Harvest Tips:

  • Harvest on dry, sunny day
  • Use hands rather than tools to avoid damage
  • Sort potatoes by size and condition
  • Set aside damaged ones for immediate use
  • Let potatoes air dry for few hours before storage

Step 6: Storage & Using Your Harvest

Proper storage keeps your potatoes fresh for months.

Storage Preparation:

  1. Cure potatoes in dark, well-ventilated area for 1-2 weeks
  2. Sort again and remove any showing signs of damage
  3. Never wash before storage (only before cooking)
  4. Store different varieties separately

Storage Conditions:

  • Temperature: 35-40°F (2-4°C)
  • Humidity: 85-90% relative humidity
  • Light: Complete darkness
  • Ventilation: Good air circulation
  • Containers: Breathable bags, wooden crates, or baskets

Storage Duration:

  • New potatoes: 1-2 weeks refrigerated
  • Cured potatoes: 4-6 months in proper storage
  • Check monthly and remove any sprouting or soft potatoes

Using Your Potatoes:

  • Boiling, mashing, and roasting
  • French fries and hash browns
  • Potato salads and gratins
  • Dehydrating for chips
  • Save best specimens for next year's seed potatoes (check local regulations)

Common Storage Problems:

  • Green potatoes: Exposed to light (contains solanine - toxic, discard)
  • Sprouting: Too warm or too much light
  • Soft spots: Too much moisture or poor ventilation
  • Sweet taste: Stored too cold (sugars develop)
