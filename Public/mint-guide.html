Mint Growing Guide

Complete Guide to Growing Mint

Learn how to grow refreshing mint in containers - easy to grow, perfect for teas, cocktails, and cooking.

Step 1: Choosing Mint Varieties

Select mint varieties based on flavor preferences and intended uses.

Popular Mint Types:

  • Spearmint: Classic mint flavor, perfect for tea and cooking
  • Peppermint: Strong, cooling flavor, medicinal uses
  • Chocolate Mint: Chocolate aroma with mint flavor
  • Apple Mint: Fuzzy leaves, fruity mint flavor

Specialty Varieties:

  • Lemon Mint: Citrusy mint for drinks and desserts
  • Orange Mint: Sweet citrus mint flavor
  • Ginger Mint: Spicy mint with ginger notes
  • Pineapple Mint: Variegated leaves, tropical flavor

Container-Friendly Features:

  • All mints grow well in containers
  • Containers actually help control spread
  • Most varieties stay under 2 feet tall
  • Hardy perennials in most climates

Why Grow Mint in Containers:

  • Invasive Control: Prevents spreading throughout garden
  • Easy Harvest: Keep plants close to kitchen
  • Winter Protection: Move indoors in cold climates
  • Variety Collection: Grow multiple types without mixing

Growth Characteristics:

  • Fast-growing and vigorous
  • Spreads by underground runners
  • Prefers cool, moist conditions
  • Can tolerate partial shade
  • Perennial in zones 3-9

Step 2: Container & Soil Setup

Prepare containers and soil to accommodate mint's vigorous growth.

Container Requirements:

  • Minimum 8-10 inches deep and wide
  • 12+ inches for larger varieties
  • Wide rather than deep containers preferred
  • Good drainage holes essential
  • Consider larger containers for spreading

Container Materials:

  • Plastic: Lightweight, retains moisture well
  • Ceramic: Attractive, good for patios
  • Fabric pots: Good drainage, air pruning of roots
  • Window boxes: Perfect for multiple varieties

Soil Mix:

  • Well-draining potting soil
  • Add compost (25% of volume)
  • pH between 6.0-7.0 (slightly acidic to neutral)
  • Rich in organic matter
  • Moisture-retentive but not waterlogged

Growing Conditions:

  • Light: 4-6 hours direct sun (tolerates partial shade)
  • Temperature: Cool to moderate, 65-75°F (18-24°C)
  • Humidity: Prefers higher humidity
  • Air Circulation: Good airflow prevents disease

Indoor Growing:

  • Bright window or grow lights
  • Cool room temperatures preferred
  • Regular misting increases humidity
  • Rotate plants for even growth

Step 3: Planting & Propagation

Start mint from cuttings, divisions, or transplants for best results.

Starting from Cuttings (Easiest):

  1. Take 4-6 inch cuttings from healthy mint plants
  2. Remove lower leaves, keep top 4-6 leaves
  3. Place cuttings in glass of water
  4. Change water every 2-3 days
  5. Roots develop in 1-2 weeks
  6. Plant when roots are 1-2 inches long

From Transplants:

  • Purchase healthy, disease-free plants from reputable sources
  • Look for vigorous, pest-free plants
  • Plant at same depth as in original pot
  • Space 12-18 inches apart in large containers

From Division:

  • Divide established mint plants in spring
  • Carefully separate root sections
  • Each division should have roots and shoots
  • Plant divisions immediately

Planting Steps:

  1. Fill container with prepared soil mix
  2. Make hole slightly larger than root ball
  3. Plant at same depth as previous container
  4. Firm soil gently around roots
  5. Water thoroughly after planting

Timing:

  • Spring: Best time for outdoor planting
  • Indoor: Any time of year
  • Fall: Take cuttings before frost
  • Avoid planting during extreme weather

Step 4: Watering & Feeding

Maintain consistent moisture and nutrition for lush mint growth.

Watering Requirements:

  • Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy
  • Mint loves water more than most herbs
  • Water when top inch of soil feels dry
  • Daily watering may be needed in hot weather
  • Reduce watering in fall and winter

Watering Tips:

  • Water deeply but avoid waterlogging
  • Water at soil level to prevent leaf diseases
  • Morning watering is best
  • Use saucers under containers to retain moisture
  • Mulch to help retain soil moisture

Fertilizing:

  • Light feeder - doesn't need heavy fertilization
  • Use diluted balanced organic liquid fertilizer
  • Feed every 3-4 weeks during growing season
  • Avoid over-fertilizing (reduces flavor)
  • Stop feeding 6 weeks before first frost

Organic Feeding Options:

  • Compost tea every 2-3 weeks
  • Diluted fish emulsion (check local regulations)
  • Worm casting tea
  • Light compost worked into soil surface
  • Liquid kelp for trace minerals

Signs of Stress:

  • Wilting: Usually needs more water
  • Yellow leaves: Overwatering or poor drainage
  • Leggy growth: Needs more light or pinching
  • Poor flavor: Too much fertilizer or water stress

Step 5: Pruning & Maintenance

Keep mint plants productive and compact through regular pruning and maintenance.

Flower Removal:

  • Pinch off flower spikes as soon as they appear
  • Flowering reduces leaf quality and plant vigor
  • Cut back to first set of leaves below flower
  • Check plants regularly during growing season
  • Some gardeners allow flowers for bees (sacrifice leaf quality)

Regular Harvesting:

  • Start harvesting when plants are 4-6 inches tall
  • Cut stems just above a leaf pair
  • Regular cutting encourages bushy growth
  • Don't take more than 1/3 of plant at once
  • Frequent light harvests better than heavy cutting

Pruning for Shape:

  • Pinch growing tips to encourage branching
  • Cut back leggy stems to promote compact growth
  • Remove any dead or damaged growth
  • Shape plants for attractive container display

Root Management:

  • Check for roots growing through drainage holes
  • Trim escaping roots to keep plant contained
  • Divide and repot every 2-3 years
  • Refresh soil when repotting

Pest and Disease Management:

  • Generally pest-free due to strong oils
  • Watch for aphids on tender new growth
  • Spider mites possible in hot, dry conditions
  • Use insecticidal soap or horticultural oil if needed
  • Ensure good air circulation to prevent fungal issues
  • Remove affected leaves immediately

Seasonal Maintenance:

  • Spring: Cut back winter damage, divide if needed
  • Summer: Regular harvesting and flower removal
  • Fall: Take cuttings, prepare for winter
  • Winter: Reduce watering, protect from frost

Step 6: Harvesting & Uses

Learn the best methods for harvesting and using fresh mint.

When to Harvest:

  • Can start harvesting 4-6 weeks after planting
  • Best flavor in morning after dew dries
  • Harvest before flowering for strongest flavor
  • Young leaves have the best taste
  • Can harvest continuously throughout growing season

Harvesting Methods:

  1. Cut stems with clean scissors or pinch with fingers
  2. Cut just above a leaf pair to encourage new growth
  3. Take entire sprigs rather than individual leaves
  4. Choose healthy, unblemished leaves
  5. Harvest in small amounts for immediate use

Fresh Storage:

  • Water storage: Place stems in water like flowers
  • Refrigerator: Wrap in damp paper towel, 1 week
  • Plastic bag: Store with paper towel in fridge
  • Don't wash until ready to use

Preservation Methods:

  • Drying: Air dry or use dehydrator (retains good flavor)
  • Freezing: Freeze whole leaves or in ice cubes
  • Oil infusion: Make mint-flavored oil (refrigerate and use within 1 week)
  • Vinegar: Create mint vinegar for salads
  • Simple syrup: Make mint syrup for drinks

Culinary Uses:

  • Fresh mint tea and iced drinks
  • Mojitos and other cocktails
  • Middle Eastern dishes like tabbouleh
  • Desserts and chocolate recipes
  • Fruit salads and yogurt
  • Mint sauce for lamb

Medicinal Uses:

  • Digestive aid after meals
  • Soothing tea for upset stomach
  • Natural breath freshener
  • Aromatherapy for mental clarity
  • Topical use for headaches (peppermint)

Winter Care & Indoor Growing:

  • Bring containers indoors before first frost
  • Place in bright window or under grow lights
  • Reduce watering and stop fertilizing
  • Harvest sparingly during winter months
  • Take cuttings to start new plants

Propagating for Next Season:

  • Take cuttings in late summer for overwintering
  • Root cuttings in water on windowsill
  • Divide large plants in spring
  • Share divisions with friends and neighbors
  • Start new varieties from cuttings
