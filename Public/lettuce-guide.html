Lettuce Growing Guide

Complete Guide to Growing Lettuce

Follow these simple steps to grow crisp, fresh lettuce in your urban garden, balcony, or windowsill.

Step 1: Choosing Lettuce Varieties

Select the right lettuce varieties for your space and growing conditions.

Common Lettuce Types:

  • Loose-leaf: Easy to grow, harvest outer leaves while plant continues growing (Oak Leaf, Red Sails, Black Seeded Simpson)
  • Butterhead: Forms loose heads with tender, sweet leaves (Bibb, Boston, Buttercrunch)
  • Romaine/Cos: Upright growth, crisp texture (Paris Island Cos, Little Gem, Rouge d'Hiver)
  • Crisphead: Forms tight heads, takes longer to mature (Iceberg, Great Lakes, Crispino)

Best Varieties for Container Growing:

  • Tom Thumb (butterhead) - miniature heads perfect for small spaces
  • Black Seeded Simpson (loose-leaf) - quick growing, heat tolerant
  • Little Gem (romaine) - small, sweet 6-inch heads
  • Red Sails (loose-leaf) - attractive red-tinged leaves
  • Salad Bowl (loose-leaf) - compact growth habit

Step 2: Planting Requirements

Get your containers, soil, and seeds ready for planting.

What You'll Need:

  • Containers at least 6 inches deep (window boxes, pots, recycled containers)
  • Quality potting mix with added compost
  • Lettuce seeds
  • Spray bottle for gentle watering

Growing Conditions:

  • Light: 4-6 hours of sunlight daily; partial shade in hot climates
  • Temperature: Cool-season crop, grows best between 45-75°F (7-24°C)
  • Soil: Well-draining, rich in organic matter, pH 6.0-7.0
  • Water: Consistent moisture; never allow to dry out completely

When to Plant:

  • Spring: 2-3 weeks before last frost date
  • Fall: 4-8 weeks before first frost date
  • Indoor/Year-round: Any time with adequate light

Step 3: Planting Methods

Plant your lettuce using one of these effective methods.

Direct Seeding:

  1. Fill container with moistened potting mix, leaving ½ inch space at the top
  2. Sprinkle seeds thinly over the surface (lettuce seeds need light to germinate)
  3. Cover very lightly with a thin layer of soil (⅛ inch or less)
  4. Mist gently with water
  5. Keep soil consistently moist until germination (usually 7-10 days)
  6. Once seedlings have 2-3 true leaves, thin to proper spacing (see below)

Spacing Guidelines:

  • Loose-leaf: 4-6 inches between plants
  • Butterhead: 6-8 inches between plants
  • Romaine: 6-8 inches between plants
  • Crisphead: 10-12 inches between plants

Succession Planting:

For a continuous harvest of lettuce, sow new seeds every 2-3 weeks throughout the growing season.

Step 4: Care & Maintenance

Proper care will ensure healthy growth and prevent common problems.

Watering:

  • Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged
  • Water when the top ½ inch of soil feels dry
  • Water at the base of plants to avoid leaf diseases
  • Morning watering is best to reduce disease risk
  • Mulch with a thin layer of compost to retain moisture

Fertilizing:

  • Apply a diluted water-soluble organic fertilizer every 2-3 weeks
  • Use an organic fertilizer with higher nitrogen content for leafy growth
  • Avoid over-fertilizing, which can lead to bitter taste

Temperature Management:

  • Protect from extreme heat (above 80°F/27°C)
  • Provide afternoon shade in hot weather
  • In cold weather, cover plants with row covers or bring containers indoors

Pest & Disease Prevention:

  • Monitor for aphids, slugs, and snails
  • Remove damaged or diseased leaves promptly
  • Ensure good air circulation between plants
  • Use universally approved methods: insecticidal soap, horticultural oil, or diatomaceous earth
  • Physical barriers like copper tape for slug control
  • Companion planting with chives or garlic to deter pests
  • For serious infestations, consult local garden centers for regionally-approved treatments

Step 5: Harvesting

Learn proper harvesting techniques to maximize your yield and extend the growing season.

When to Harvest:

  • Baby Greens: 3-4 weeks after planting when leaves are 2-4 inches tall
  • Full-sized Leaves: 5-6 weeks after planting
  • Heads: When heads feel firm (varies by variety, 6-8 weeks for most)
  • Harvest in the morning when leaves are crisp and full of moisture

Harvesting Methods:

  • Cut-and-Come-Again: Harvest outer leaves when they reach 3-4 inches, leaving the center to continue growing
  • Whole Plant: Cut entire plant 1 inch above soil level (many varieties will regrow)
  • Head Lettuce: Cut the entire head at soil level when firm

Tools for Harvesting:

  • Clean, sharp scissors or garden shears
  • Harvest basket or container
  • Clean hands for delicate varieties

Step 6: Storage & Using Your Harvest

Proper storage will keep your lettuce fresh and crisp for longer.

Washing & Storing:

  1. Gently rinse leaves in cool water to remove dirt and insects
  2. Dry thoroughly using a salad spinner or clean kitchen towels
  3. Wrap in paper towels to absorb excess moisture
  4. Place in a perforated plastic bag or container in the refrigerator
  5. Store in the crisper drawer for 7-10 days (varies by variety)

Using Your Lettuce:

  • Fresh salads with homemade dressing
  • Lettuce wraps as a bread alternative
  • Add to sandwiches and burgers
  • Wilt into soups and stir-fries (especially heartier romaine)
  • Juice with other vegetables for nutritious green juice

Saving Seeds:

If you'd like to save seeds for next season:

  1. Allow one or two plants to "bolt" (flower)
  2. Let seed heads develop and begin to dry on the plant
  3. Carefully cut dried seed heads and place in paper bag
  4. Allow to dry completely for 1-2 weeks
  5. Separate seeds from chaff and store in cool, dry place
