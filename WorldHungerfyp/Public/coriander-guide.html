Coriander Growing Guide

Complete Guide to Growing Coriander

Learn how to grow this versatile herb - fresh leaves (cilantro) for cooking and seeds (coriander) for spice.

Step 1: Understanding Coriander

Learn about this dual-purpose herb that provides both fresh leaves and aromatic seeds.

Names and Uses:

  • Cilantro: Fresh leaves used in cooking
  • Coriander: Dried seeds used as spice
  • Chinese Parsley: Another name for fresh leaves
  • Same plant (Coriandrum sativum) provides both products

Varieties to Choose:

  • Slow Bolt: Slower to flower, longer leaf harvest
  • Santo: Heat-tolerant, good for warm climates
  • Leisure: Compact, perfect for containers
  • Calypso: Slow bolting, uniform growth

Growth Characteristics:

  • Fast-growing annual herb
  • Cool-season crop, bolts in heat
  • Grows 12-24 inches tall
  • Delicate, feathery leaves
  • Small white or pink flowers

Container Growing Benefits:

  • Easy succession planting
  • Control over growing conditions
  • Protection from extreme heat
  • Fresh herbs always available
  • Can grow year-round indoors

Culinary Uses:

  • Fresh leaves: Mexican, Asian, and Indian dishes
  • Seeds: Spice blends, pickling, baking
  • Roots: Used in Thai cooking
  • Essential ingredient in salsa and curry

Step 2: Container & Soil Preparation

Set up containers and soil for optimal coriander growth and harvest.

Container Requirements:

  • Minimum 6-8 inches deep for leaf harvest
  • 10-12 inches deep for seed production
  • 6-8 inch diameter for single plant
  • Good drainage holes essential
  • Window boxes great for succession planting

Container Options:

  • Small pots: 6-inch for microgreens and young leaves
  • Medium pots: 8-10 inch for full plants
  • Window boxes: Multiple plantings, staggered harvest
  • Shallow trays: Perfect for microgreen production

Soil Mix:

  • Well-draining potting soil
  • Add compost (20% of volume)
  • pH between 6.0-7.0 (neutral)
  • Light, loose texture for good drainage
  • Rich in organic matter

Growing Conditions:

  • Light: 4-6 hours direct sun (tolerates partial shade)
  • Temperature: Cool weather preferred, 50-75¬∞F (10-24¬∞C)
  • Air Circulation: Good airflow prevents disease
  • Protection: Shade from hot afternoon sun

Seasonal Considerations:

  • Spring/Fall: Best growing seasons
  • Summer: Provide afternoon shade
  • Winter: Grow indoors or in greenhouse
  • Bolts quickly in hot weather

Step 3: Seeding & Succession Planting

Master succession planting techniques for continuous fresh cilantro harvest.

Seed Preparation:

  • Use fresh coriander seeds for best germination
  • Lightly crush seeds to improve germination
  • Soak seeds 24 hours before planting (optional)
  • Seeds remain viable for 2-3 years

Direct Seeding Steps:

  1. Fill containers with prepared soil mix
  2. Sow seeds ¬º to ¬Ω inch deep
  3. Space seeds 1-2 inches apart
  4. Cover lightly with soil
  5. Water gently to avoid displacing seeds
  6. Keep soil consistently moist

Succession Planting Strategy:

  • Plant new seeds every 2-3 weeks
  • Use multiple containers for continuous harvest
  • Start new plantings before previous ones bolt
  • Plant smaller amounts more frequently
  • Adjust timing based on season and climate

Germination:

  • Germination takes 7-14 days
  • Keep soil consistently moist but not soggy
  • Optimal soil temperature 60-70¬∞F (15-21¬∞C)
  • Seedlings emerge with characteristic divided leaves

Timing:

  • Spring: Start 2-3 weeks before last frost
  • Summer: Plant in partial shade, frequent watering
  • Fall: Best growing season in most areas
  • Winter: Indoor growing with supplemental light

Thinning:

  • Thin seedlings when 2-3 inches tall
  • Leave strongest plants 4-6 inches apart
  • Use thinned seedlings as microgreens
  • Don't transplant - direct seeding works best

Step 4: Watering & Feeding

Maintain proper moisture and nutrition for healthy, flavorful growth.

Watering Guidelines:

  • Keep soil consistently moist but not waterlogged
  • Water when top inch of soil feels dry
  • More frequent watering in hot weather
  • Reduce watering if growing for seeds
  • Water at soil level to prevent leaf diseases

Watering Tips:

  • Use gentle watering to avoid disturbing shallow roots
  • Morning watering reduces disease risk
  • Ensure good drainage to prevent root rot
  • Container plants dry out faster than garden plants
  • Mulch lightly to retain moisture

Fertilizing:

  • Light feeder - avoid over-fertilizing
  • Use diluted balanced organic liquid fertilizer
  • Feed every 3-4 weeks during growing season
  • Too much nitrogen delays flowering (good for leaves)
  • Reduce feeding if growing for seeds

Organic Feeding Options:

  • Compost tea every 2-3 weeks
  • Diluted fish emulsion (check local regulations)
  • Worm casting tea
  • Light application of compost
  • Liquid kelp for trace minerals

Heat Stress Management:

  • Provide afternoon shade in hot weather
  • Increase watering frequency
  • Move containers to cooler locations
  • Use reflective mulch to reduce soil temperature
  • Group containers together for microclimate

Signs of Problems:

  • Wilting: Usually needs more water or shade
  • Yellow leaves: Overwatering or poor drainage
  • Premature bolting: Too much heat or stress
  • Slow growth: Insufficient light or nutrients

Step 5: Managing Bolting & Flowering

Learn to control bolting for leaf harvest or encourage it for seed production.

Understanding Bolting:

  • Natural response to heat, long days, or stress
  • Plant sends up flower stalks to produce seeds
  • Leaf quality declines once flowering begins
  • Inevitable part of plant's life cycle

Delaying Bolting for Leaf Harvest:

  • Choose slow-bolt varieties
  • Grow in cool seasons (spring/fall)
  • Provide afternoon shade in summer
  • Keep soil consistently moist
  • Harvest regularly to reduce stress

Encouraging Flowering for Seeds:

  • Allow plants to experience some stress
  • Reduce watering slightly
  • Stop harvesting leaves
  • Provide full sun
  • Let plants reach maturity

Flower Care:

  • Small white or pink umbrella-shaped flowers
  • Flowers attract beneficial insects
  • Support tall flower stems if needed
  • Protect from strong winds
  • Continue light watering during flowering

Seed Development:

  • Seeds form 4-6 weeks after flowering
  • Green seeds turn brown when mature
  • Seeds are ready when they rattle in pods
  • Harvest before seeds fall naturally

Succession Strategy:

  • Dedicate some plants for leaf harvest
  • Allow others to go to seed
  • Start new plantings as others bolt
  • Stagger plantings every 2-3 weeks
  • Use different varieties for different purposes

Step 6: Harvesting & Uses

Learn optimal harvesting techniques for both fresh leaves and dried seeds.

Harvesting Cilantro (Leaves):

  • Start harvesting when plants are 4-6 inches tall
  • Cut outer leaves first, leaving center to grow
  • Harvest in morning after dew dries
  • Use clean scissors or pinch with fingers
  • Regular harvesting encourages new growth

Leaf Harvesting Tips:

  • Take only what you need for immediate use
  • Younger leaves have better flavor
  • Stop harvesting once flowers appear
  • Cut stems just above a leaf pair
  • Don't take more than 1/3 of plant at once

Harvesting Coriander Seeds:

  1. Wait until seeds turn brown and dry
  2. Test seeds - they should rattle in pods
  3. Cut entire seed heads in morning
  4. Place in paper bags to continue drying
  5. Shake bags to release seeds
  6. Sift to remove chaff

Processing Seeds:

  • Dry seeds completely before storage
  • Store in airtight containers
  • Label with harvest date
  • Grind just before use for best flavor
  • Whole seeds keep longer than ground

Fresh Storage Methods:

  • Water storage: Place stems in water like flowers
  • Refrigerator: Wrap in damp paper towel, 1 week
  • Plastic bag: Store with paper towel in fridge
  • Freezing: Freeze whole leaves or in ice cubes

Cilantro Uses:

  • Fresh garnish for Mexican and Asian dishes
  • Essential ingredient in salsa and guacamole
  • Add to curries and stir-fries
  • Blend into chimichurri sauce
  • Use in fresh herb salads
  • Make cilantro lime rice

Coriander Seed Uses:

  • Spice blends like garam masala
  • Pickling spice for vegetables
  • Baking bread and pastries
  • Curry powders and pastes
  • Marinades for meat and fish
  • Herbal teas for digestion

Microgreen Production:

  • Harvest seedlings at 1-2 inches tall
  • Cut just above soil level
  • Ready in 10-14 days from seeding
  • Intense flavor in small package
  • Perfect for salads and garnishes

Year-Round Growing:

  • Indoor growing with bright light
  • Succession planting every 2-3 weeks
  • Cool season crops in heated greenhouse
  • Save seeds for next season's planting
  • Try different varieties for extended harvest
